*King Grohl*

*Quarterback – Grade: A+*

What do the top teams share in common? They all drafted stud QB’s with
their first two picks. Tom went *Jalen Hurts *(5) and *Lamar Jackson* (20)
in rounds 1 and 2 and I’m starting to think that they edge out George save
for the performance/injury issues that *Lamar Jackson *had last year. Tom
and George are 1.A and 1.B in my book on this position

   - *Jalen Hurts *is a top-3 QB that comes in just under Josh Allen and
   Patrick Mahomes. What *Hurts* lacks as a passer, he makes up for in his
   rushing attempts and TD’s scored last year making him a top fantasy scorer.
   - *Lamar Jackson *has been more boom/bust in recent years so I have some
   reservations. Regardless, the equalizer on this pick is his rushing.
   Furthermore, I like the weapons this year with Mark Andrews, OBJ, and Zay
   Flowers. Tom has a QB1 he is putting in his QB2 slot
   - *Kyler Murray: *I’m not sure what the future holds for *Kyler Murray*
   and his rehab from his ACL tear at the end of last year, but he is worth a
   flyer on the IR spot. Smart

*Running Back – Grade: B*

   - *The good news:* Tom’s starting RB’s are *Josh Jacobs, Kenneth Walker
   III, *and *David Montgomery*.
      - I love what *Josh Jacobs* did last year
      - I love what *Kenneth Walker III *did after Penney went down
      - I love that *David Montgomery* was a volume back in Chicago
   - *The bad news: *Tom’s bench RB’s are *D’Andre Swift, Kareem Hunt,
   Tyjae Spears, Gus Edwards *all of whom are behind on the depth chart or
   involved in an RB committee
      - I worry that *Josh Jacobs *mooned last year as RB3 on the season
      and is coming back down to earth this year
      - I worry that *Kenneth Walker III* will share much of the workload
      with Zach Charbonnet, lowering his ceiling
      - I worry that *David Montgomery *will share much of his workload
      with the even more talented and exciting Jamyr Gibbs, lowering
his ceiling

*Wide Receiver – Grade: B+*

I like these receivers and the upside that they bring to the table for
Tom’s team. *DK Metcalf, Diontae Johnson, Christian Watson, George Pickens,
*and *Brandon Cooks. *Specifically:

   - *DK Metcalf *has a great floor under him based on his 141 targets from
   last year. If he can improve the efficiency numbers, we’re looking at him
   moving on up from WR2 to WR1 territory for the year
   - *Christian Watson* coupled with my favorable view on Jordan Love makes
   him an exciting break out candidate
   - *Johnson *and *Pickens* are upside guys in my opinion with Johnson

*Dreorge Bledsoe*

*Quarterback – Grade: A+*

George might have the best 2-QB combo in the league with his strong picks
of *Burrow *and *Fields *that were both taken in the first two rounds of

associate with *Justin Fields* as a young mobile QB

   - *Joe Burrow: *The few knocks I have on *Burrow *come from some of the
   injury concerns that have popped up in training camp, and him on the cusp
   of being an “elite” QB due to his lack of relative rushing upside you get
   with the other top guys. He’s a safe pick, with low risk. I’m
expecting *Burrow
   *to continue and improve his strong performance this year behind a
   healthier O-line and Ja’Marr Chase

   - *Justin Fields*: I love this pick for the sheer upside. He’s a riskier

   phenomenal and if they can be sustained this year this is going to look
   like a top-5 pick. Look for improvements in the passing game with the
   addition of DJ Moore

*Running Back – Grade: B+*

Of the few knocks that I can make on George’s roster, I want it to be the
running backs. When I look at that packed stable of backs, I remind myself
that I need to give credit where it’s due. No stellar elite names here,
just some strong Clydesdales that will hopefully help pull the team the
distance in this year’s competition

   - What George gave up taking 2 top QB’s and a top WR in the first 3
   rounds of this draft, he made up for by pursuing a deep running back bench
   that has potential
   - The one’s I’m most excited about are *Travis Etienne, James Cook, J.K.

   the tank under BB and is a good flyer as a late round pick)

area of greatest concern. I love the starting 3 WR’s but bring the grade

WR’s of *Jaylen Waddle, Amari Cooper, *and *Mike Williams*.

   - *Waddle* and *Cooper* finished 7th and 9th in the league last year
   - *Williams* brings up the rear finishing 31st due to time missed while
   injured. His injuries concern me, but are stabilized by the expectation of
   a healthy Justin Herbert who has the weapons to throw 50 TD’s this year
   (you heard it here first)
   - My first real knock on Geroge’s team goes to the lack of WR depth
with *Allen
   Lazard* as his only WR on the bench
